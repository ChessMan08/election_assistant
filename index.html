How Elections Work in India

Click on each step to learn more. Follow the journey from announcement to results.

01
📣

Election Announcement

The Election Commission officially announces the election date.

+
  • The Election Commission of India (ECI) schedules the election after consulting with state governments.
  • The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) kicks in immediately — limiting what ruling parties can do.
  • Dates for nominations, voting, and results are announced simultaneously.
  • This happens roughly 4–6 weeks before polling day.
💡 Tip: Once the MCC is in effect, the government cannot announce new schemes or make policy promises.
02
📝

Voter Registration

Check if your name is on the Electoral Roll.

+
  • Every Indian citizen aged 18 or above is eligible to vote.
  • Check your voter registration on voters.eci.gov.in or the Voter Helpline App.
  • If not registered, fill Form 6 online at the National Voter's Service Portal (NVSP).
  • Your Voter ID Card (EPIC) is the primary ID for voting.
  • You can also use Aadhaar, Passport, or Driving License as alternate photo IDs.
💡 Tip: Registration closes well before the election. Always check early — deadlines can be 30+ days before polling.
03
🙋

Candidate Nominations

Candidates file papers to officially contest the election.

+
  • Parties and independents file nomination papers before the deadline set by ECI.
  • Candidates must pay a security deposit (₹25,000 for Lok Sabha, ₹10,000 for state assembly).
  • Scrutiny of nominations happens the day after the filing deadline.
  • Candidates can withdraw their nominations within a specified window.
  • The final list of contesting candidates is published.
💡 Tip: Candidates who get less than 1/6th of total votes lose their security deposit!
04
📢

Campaign Period

Candidates and parties campaign to win your vote.

+
  • Campaigns involve rallies, ads, door-to-door visits, social media, and public speeches.
  • All spending must be within limits: ₹95 lakh for Lok Sabha, ₹40 lakh for assembly (varies by state).
  • Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling day — this is called the "Silent Period."
  • No distribution of cash, alcohol, or gifts — this is a punishable offence.
💡 Tip: You can report campaign violations on the cVIGIL app by ECI — upload photos or videos as evidence!
05
🗳️

Polling Day

The day you cast your vote at your local booth.

+
  • Locate your polling booth using your Voter ID or the ECI website.
  • Bring a valid photo ID. Booths are typically open from 7 AM to 6 PM.
  • You'll press a button on the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) next to your chosen candidate's name and symbol.
  • After voting, your finger is marked with indelible ink on the left index finger.
  • A VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) slip shows your vote for 7 seconds for verification.
💡 Tip: Your employer is legally required to give you paid leave on election day. It's your right!
06
📊

Counting & Results

Votes are counted and the winner is declared.

+
  • Counting typically happens 1–2 days after polling closes across all phases.
  • EVM results are matched with VVPAT slips for verification in selected booths.
  • The candidate with the most votes (First Past The Post system) wins.
  • Results are declared on ECI's website in real-time as counting proceeds.
  • The winning party's leader is invited by the President to form the government.
💡 Tip: India uses a "First Past The Post" system — you don't need a majority, just more votes than anyone else in your constituency.

Election Timeline

A typical Lok Sabha election follows this schedule from announcement to swearing-in.

Day 1

📣 Official Announcement

Election Commission announces dates, activates Model Code of Conduct.

~6 weeks before polling
Day 3–7

📝 Nomination Filing Opens

Candidates submit nomination papers to Returning Officers in their constituencies.

Window stays open for ~1 week
Day 8

🔍 Scrutiny of Nominations

Election officers verify that all nomination papers are valid and complete.

Invalid papers are rejected here
Day 10

✂️ Withdrawal Deadline

Last day for candidates to withdraw. Final list of contestants published.

Ballot paper order is set
Day 11–38

📢 Campaign Period

Parties and candidates hold rallies, ads, and outreach to voters.

Spending limits strictly enforced
Day 38

🤫 Silent Period Begins

All campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling day. No speeches, ads or canvassing.

MCC strictly enforced here
Day 40

🗳️ Polling Day — VOTE!

Booths open 7AM–6PM. Bring your Voter ID. Press the EVM button. Get inked. Done!

Your most important civic action
Day 42–45

📊 Vote Counting & Results

EVMs are opened, votes counted, VVPAT verified. Winners declared constituency-by-constituency.

Results updated live on ECI website
Day 50–60

🏛️ Government Formation

Winning party/alliance leader meets the President. Sworn in as Prime Minister. Cabinet formed.

New government takes charge
Note: Lok Sabha elections are held in multiple phases (up to 7 phases across different states). The timeline above shows a single-phase constituency. Some states vote on different dates.
